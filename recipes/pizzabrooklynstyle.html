Pizza Brooklyn Style

This simple brick-oven pizza recipe has been made famous by several well-known, Brooklyn wood-fire pizzerias.

This recipe assumes you already have a pizza base or dough.

Servings:2 Pizzas

Ingredients

  • 6 ounces low moisture mozzarella cheese, thinly sliced
  • ½ teaspoon dried oregano
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • ½ cup no salt added canned crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 6 leaves fresh basil, torn

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven, with a pizza stone on the lowest rack, to 550 degrees F (288 degrees C).

    Lightly dust a pizza peel with flour.

  2. Put pizza base on peel.

    Lay 1/2 of the mozzarella on the crust, then sprinkle 1/4 teaspoon oregano and 1/8 teaspoon pepper over the top.

    Randomly arrange 1/4 cup crushed tomatoes over the pizza, leaving some empty areas, then drizzle 1 tablespoon olive oil over top.


  3. Place the tip of the peel at the back of the preheated pizza stone, and remove the peel so that the pizza is left on the stone.

  4. Bake in the preheated oven until the crust begins to brown, 4 to 6 minutes.

  5. Slide the pizza onto the peel and remove from the oven; then slide off the peel onto a work surface.

    Sprinkle 1/2 of the basil leaves randomly over the pizza.

    Cut into wedges and serve.


  6. Follow the steps again to make a second pizza
